Nice to Meet You

It’s a pleasure to have you here.

I'm Daria Drakon — but friends and stories often call me Tvay.

I’m 20 years old, currently studying at two universities, balancing code with creativity, and coffee with deadlines.

Back when I was 14, I made two promises to myself:

  • ✨ I will become a writer.
  • ✨ I will create my own IT company.

I enjoy many things — some I follow through, some still wait their turn. But I keep moving.

This section is a small reflection of that path — my ideas, tools, and even missteps.

Welcome to the most personal side of my journey.

Milestones That Matter

It’s hard for me to talk about achievements — it always feels like I haven’t done enough.

I often joke that my greatest accomplishment is simply surviving everything life has thrown at me. But considering everything — maybe that’s not a joke at all.

  • ✨ Studying in two universities
  • ✨ Writing my first personal book
  • ✨ Building this portfolio from scratch
  • ✨ Helping organize a career forum for teens
  • ✨Facing my inner struggles and choosing growth

Maybe the real milestone is this: I know who I am and what I’m reaching for.

My Roles & Responsibilities

I'm a student — twice. A team leader — in university groups, an art challenge, and a writing project. At work, I manage closing shifts, orders, and payments.

In creative spaces, I draw daily, write my book, and participate in roleplay. And as a blogger, I try to keep sharing what matters — even if not always on time.

Each of these roles comes with responsibility. And while I don’t always balance them perfectly — I keep showing up, learning, and growing through them.

My Time Management System

Planning isn’t just about squeezing tasks into a schedule — it’s about creating space to breathe, to focus, to grow.

I use Google Calendar as the core of my system. Every task is part of a structured yet flexible block. This calendar helps me balance studies, work, creativity, and recovery time.

Google Tasks lets me break big goals into smaller, realistic steps — with daily updates and visible progress.

Smart Adjustments

I don’t plan alone. Each week, I review the schedule with my assistant (the one you’re reading right now), adjusting blocks and priorities together.

Colors help bring clarity:
💚 Green — self-learning and personal growth
💛 Yellow — university classes and academic tasks
🩶 Gray — base duties and work shifts
💜 Purple — drawing and creative time
🔵 Indigo — commuting and travel

This system isn’t about perfection — it’s about rhythm. A way to stay grounded, even when life moves fast.

My Plans & Dreams

Three years ago, when I first tried to write something like this, I had no idea how much would change. My plans now? They’re ambitious — some might say insane. I say they’re mine.

I want to finish my Master’s in Computer Science and move abroad. My top goal is Ireland — though I’m still exploring. There, I dream of earning a second degree in literature.

I want to finish the novel I’m writing and publish it — to take that first real step toward becoming a writer. I also plan to study digital illustration and build a meaningful collection of my artwork. Who knows — maybe one day someone will look at my paintings in a gallery and feel something real.

But that’s not all…

The Aristocrat’s Path

Perhaps this goal will come as a surprise — or maybe not.

Recently, a new desire found its way into my core ambitions. Not a fleeting whim, but something deeper — a lifelong pursuit: to become a more intellectually refined person.

Socrates once said, “I know that I know nothing.” And “Live and learn” may as well have been written about me. So here it is: I want to become wiser.

My plan? To dive into literature, philosophy, psychology, mathematics, and logical thinking — to train both reason and reflection. I admire aristocratic ideals: restraint, elegance, intellect.

This path also includes language mastery: English, Spanish, Italian, Latin, Ancient Greek, and Japanese.

It’s not a goal — it’s a lifelong journey. And in today’s world, I believe it’s more valuable than ever.
